About Homestuck

Homestuck is a multimedia webcomic created by Andrew Hussie, which ran for 7 years from 2009 to 2016. During its lifetime, it had been contributed to by dozens of artists, produced over 8000 pages, including music from 10 soundtrack albums, and amassed a following of millions of fans.

It is hard to encompass the story of the comic in a short summary. It's about four kids playing a game that causes the end of the world, but its premise unravels into one of complexity so much higher than that, sending the characters throughout time, space, the multiverse, and the literal fourth wall. I don't want to spoil the plot for you any further than that. Just know you will not find any other comic like it ever, so read while you still have the chance.

Where To Read Homestuck

Officially Supported

  • Homestuck.com is the official web mirror of Homestuck and MS Paint Adventures. The archive is currently incomplete, but is constantly being updated with new content and features. Currently the only mirror of Homestuck with alt text.
  • The Unofficial Homestuck Collection is an unofficial desktop application reader of Homestuck, MSPA, as well as Andrew Hussie's entire old catalogue. It was made unavailable by its original owner, but is available to download officially on the Homestuck GitHub.

Other Mirrors

  • MSPA To Go is a mobile optimized version of Homestuck. Currently the only one with mobile controls for all the interactive flashes. Also has archives of all the other MS Paint Adventures.

I finished Homestuck - What next?

Stories In The Homestuck Universe

  • The Homestuck Epilogues is the official dubiously canon continuation to Homestuck. Its story is still ongoing through Homestuck^2 and Beyond Canon.
  • Paradox Space is a series of non-canon comics outside of the alpha timeline. Various doomed timeline tales, random shenanigans, and stories theorizing on gaps that the comic left behind. Highlights include Summerteen Romance and The Inaugural Death of Mister Seven.
